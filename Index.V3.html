Distribution Network & Landed Cost Optimization

Capstone / Network Design

This case asks for the most cost-effective operating model across two DFW distribution centers, including inbound mode choice, ZIP code assignment, and the optimal full-time versus overtime labor mix.

Total landed cost ZIP reassignment Labor modeling Profit margin impact
  • What needs to be done: build the current-state cost model, compare delivered versus 3PL inbound, reassign regions by cheapest origin DC, test labor scenarios, and quantify savings and margin lift.
  • Technical skills: Excel or Python modeling, scenario analysis, cost-per-case design, optimization logic, workforce capacity calculations, and executive presentation.
  • Best portfolio framing: show a baseline-versus-optimized dashboard, a landed-cost waterfall, and a concise recommendation panel.

Spare Parts Forecasting & Safety Stock Strategy

Forecasting / Service Levels

Dockomo’s spare-parts business faces an unusual combination of higher inventory, lower availability, weak first-pick performance, and erratic demand across a very large SKU base.

Demand segmentation Service level design Lead-time risk Reorder points
  • What needs to be done: segment SKUs by forecastability and movement, use different forecasting logic by category, redesign safety stock without relying on normality, and create reorder policies that respect long vendor lead times.
  • Technical skills: time-series analysis, intermittent demand thinking, inventory policy design, service-level modeling, distribution diagnostics, and ERP/SAP process understanding.
  • Best portfolio framing: present the case as a structured uncertainty problem with a forecasting framework, safety stock method, and SKU decision tree.

EOQ, Reorder Point, and Inventory Policy Trade-offs

Inventory Control

The Silo Manufacturing case is a strong example of classical inventory control: balancing carrying cost, ordering cost, order quantity, and operational practicality such as whole-case ordering.

EOQ Ordering cost Carrying cost Robustness
  • What needs to be done: compare proposed order sizes, calculate EOQ, evaluate the nearest whole-case alternative, and explain the cost sensitivity of deviating from the theoretical optimum.
  • Technical skills: inventory math, model assumptions, sensitivity testing, reorder point calculations, and translating formulas into business guidance.
  • Best portfolio framing: use a clean comparison table and a short insight such as “close-to-optimal policies can be operationally superior with minimal cost penalty.”

How to narrate these cases on your portfolio

The goal is not to upload class assignments as-is. The goal is to turn each one into a recruiter-friendly story that proves analytical maturity. That means giving each project the same structure and trimming away classroom-only detail.

1. Lead with the business question

Context first

Use titles like “Optimizing Landed Cost Across Two Distribution Centers” instead of course names. Recruiters care more about the problem solved than the class it came from.

2. Show your decision workflow

Method second

Include a short section for data inputs, analysis steps, assumptions, and model logic. This is where you demonstrate technical credibility without overwhelming the page.

3. Publish outputs, not just narration

Visual proof

Use one or two visuals per case: cost comparison, scenario table, demand segmentation graphic, safety stock framework, or EOQ trade-off chart. Your current site needs more evidence and fewer general paragraphs.

4. End with a recommendation

Executive finish

Every case should close with a concise recommendation statement, what changed, why it matters, and what trade-offs were accepted. That makes the work feel business-ready.
